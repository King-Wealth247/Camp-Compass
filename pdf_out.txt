1. Introduction 
1.1 Project Overview 
This document constitutes the formal Cahier des Charges (Software Requirements 
Specification) for the University Campus Mapping and Timetable Management System, 
named Camp -Compass (UCMTMS). It describes the functional and non -functional 
requirements, syste m architecture, actors, constraints, and technical stack for the 
development of the application. 
The Application name is Camp-Compass.  
 
1.2 Project Objectives 
The UCMTMS aims to provide an integrated digital platform that fulfills two primary functions: 
• Interactive Campus Mapping: Provide accurate, interactive maps of university 
campuses including outdoor navigation via Google Maps integration and indoor 
navigation via interactive floor plan drawings for each building. 
• Automated Timetable Generation and Viewing: Automate the creation of weekly 
academic timetables, taking into account courses, lecturer availability, hall capacities, 
and scheduling constraints, while providing role-specific views for students and staff. 
 
1.3 Scope 
The application is designed for deployment in: 
• Universities and higher education institutions 
• Professional training centers 
• Large secondary schools 
 
The system shall support multi -campus institutions, including campuses located in different 
cities and regions within the same country. The application shall be accessible via web 
browsers and mobile devices (iOS and Android), and shall function in both on line and offline 
modes.

3. Functional Requirements 
3.1 Authentication and User Management 
3.1.1 Login 
• All users (Students, Staff, Admin, Registrar) shall authenticate using an institutional 
email address and an auto-generated password. 
• Credentials are generated by the Registrar upon entry of a new member into the 
institution's system. 
• Users shall be able to change their auto-generated password on first login. 
 
3.1.2 Access Control 
• The system shall enforce role-based access control (RBAC) for all features. 
• Students with an unpaid first tuition instalment shall only have access to the campus 
mapping module. All other features (timetable, notifications) shall be locked until 
payment is confirmed. 
• Staff members shall access their personal schedule and the mapping module. 
• Admin and Registrar actors shall have access to their respective management 
dashboards. 
 
3.1.3 Account Profile and Detail Correction Request 
● All users (Students, Staff, Admin, and Registrar) shall be able to view their own 
account details within a dedicated Profile section of the application. The profile shall 
display information including full name, institutional email, role, department or faculty, 
and academic level where applicable. 
● Users shall not be able to edit their account details directly. If a user identifies an 
error in their profile — such as a misspelling of their name or an incorrect department 
or faculty attribution — they may submit a formal Detail Correction Request through 
the application. 
● When submitting a Detail Correction Request, the user shall be required to provide 
the following: 
A clear description of the incorrect information and the correct value being requested. 
Supporting evidence in the form of one or more of the following: an official identity or 
academic document (e.g., national ID, student card, or enrolment certificate), a payment 
receipt, or an employment contract. Evidence shall be uploaded as an image or PDF copy 
directly within the request form. 
● Submitted correction requests shall be routed to an Admin for review and validation. 
The Admin shall be able to approve or reject the request from the administration 
dashboard, optionally providing a reason for rejection. 
● Upon Admin approval, the relevant account detail shall be updated in the system 
automatically. The user shall receive an in-app notification confirming whether their 
request was approved or rejected. 
● A user may only have one pending correction request at a time per field. Uploaded 
evidence documents shall be stored securely and accessible only to Admin users. 
3.2 Campus Mapping Module

• The system shall display interactive maps of all campuses of the institution using the 
Google Maps API. 
• Each campus shall be identifiable and selectable, even if located in different cities or 
regions. 
• The map shall display buildings, entrances, parking areas, and key points of interest 
with labelled markers. 
• Users shall be able to search for specific buildings or locations within a campus. 
 
3.2.2 Indoor Floor Plans 
• Each building shall have one or more interactive floor plan drawings, one per floor. 
• Floor plans shall be displayed as interactive image-based drawings (SVG or 
annotated raster images). 
• Users shall be able to navigate between floors within a building. 
• Rooms and halls shall be labelled and clickable, showing details such as room name, 
capacity, and type. 
• The system shall support linking a timetable entry directly to its hall's location on the 
floor plan (see section 3.3.6). 
 
3.2.3 Multi-Campus Support 
• The system shall support institutions with multiple campuses across different cities 
and regions. 
• A campus selector shall allow users to switch between campuses. 
• All mapping data shall be stored and retrieved on a per-campus basis. 
 
3.3 Timetable Management Module 
3.3.1 Timetable Generation 
• The Admin shall be able to trigger weekly timetable generation. 
• The system shall automatically generate timetables taking into account the following 
constraints: 
○ Courses assigned to each department and academic level. 
○ Lecturer availability for the week (as declared by lecturers). 
○ Hall capacity relative to the number of students in the class. 
○ Hall type (e.g., laboratory, lecture theatre, seminar room) matching the course 
requirements. 
○ Absence of scheduling conflicts: a hall cannot be assigned to two courses at the same 
time slot. 
○ Absence of lecturer conflicts: a lecturer cannot teach two courses simultaneously.  
 
3.3.2 Timetable Viewing — Students 
• Students shall view timetables filtered exclusively to their enrolled department and 
academic level. 
• No cross-departmental or cross-level timetable data shall be visible to students. 
• The timetable shall display: day, time slot, course name, lecturer name, and hall 
designation.

3.3.3 Timetable Viewing — Staff 
• Staff members who are lecturers shall view their personal weekly teaching schedule. 
• Non-teaching staff shall have access to general campus timetable information 
relevant to their role. 
 
3.3.4 Lecturer Availability Declaration 
• Lecturers shall declare their availability for the following week before a configurable 
weekly deadline. 
• Lecturers shall also be permitted to edit their availability mid-week (e.g., due to 
unforeseen circumstances). 
• Any mid-week availability change shall trigger an automatic notification to the Admin. 
• The Admin shall manually update the affected timetable entries accordingly. 
• Students of the impacted department and level shall be notified of the timetable 
change. 
 
3.3.5 Hall Management 
• The Admin shall manage the list of halls, including their name, campus, building, 
floor, capacity, and type. 
• Halls shall be marked as available or unavailable for scheduling. 
• The system shall prevent double-booking of halls. 
 
3.3.6 Hall Search 
● The system shall provide a dedicated Hall Search interface accessible to Admin 
users and, in read-only mode, to authorised Staff members. 
● Users shall be able to search for halls using one or more of the following criteria: 
Hall Code: unique identifier assigned to each hall (e.g., “A-101”). 
Building Code: identifier of the building in which the hall is located (e.g., “BLD-03”). 
Hall Name: full or partial name of the hall. 
Hall Type: lecture theatre, laboratory, seminar room, etc. 
Campus: filter halls by campus. 
Capacity: minimum seating capacity required. 
Availability Status: available or unavailable for scheduling. 
● Search criteria may be combined to perform multi-criteria filtering. Results shall 
update in real time as criteria are entered. 
● Each result in the hall search list shall be clickable, directing the user to the hall’s 
details page and its location on the relevant floor plan. 
● The backend shall expose a dedicated REST endpoint (e.g., GET /api/halls/search) 
accepting query parameters for all supported search criteria. The Python backend 
layer shall implement the filtering logic using parameterised database queries to 
ensure security and performance. 
3.3.7 Map Integration from Timetable 
• From any timetable entry, a student or staff member shall be able to click on the hall 
name to view its location on the campus map. 
• The system shall navigate to the correct campus, building, and floor plan, highlighting 
the relevant hall.

3.4 Notification Module 
• The system shall support in-app and push notifications. 
• Notification triggers include: 
○ Timetable changes affecting a student's department and level. 
○ Lecturer availability updates received by the Admin. 
○ System announcements from the Admin. 
• Approval or rejection of a user’s Detail Correction Request (see section 3.1.3). 
• Students shall only receive notifications relevant to their department and level. 
 
3.5 Registration and Onboarding Module 
• The Registrar shall register new students and staff into the system. 
• Upon registration, the system shall auto-generate an institutional email address and 
a temporary password. 
• Registration details shall include: full name, role, department (for students), 
faculty/department (for staff), and academic level (for students). 
• A student's access tier (restricted vs. standard) shall be linked to their tuition fee 
payment status.

4. Non-Functional Requirements 
4.1 Availability and Offline Mode 
• The application shall function in both online and offline modes using Progressive 
Web Application (PWA) techniques and local data caching. 
• Campus maps and the most recently fetched timetable shall be accessible offline. 
• Data synchronisation shall occur automatically when connectivity is restored. 
 
4.2 Cross-Platform Compatibility 
• The application shall be accessible via all major web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, 
Edge, Safari). 
• The application shall be installable on Android and iOS devices as a PWA. 
• The user interface shall be fully responsive and optimised for mobile screens. 
 
4.3 Performance 
• Map tiles and floor plan images shall load within 3 seconds on a standard 4G mobile 
connection. 
• Timetable generation shall complete within 30 seconds for a standard institution 
configuration. 
• The application shall support a minimum of 5,000 concurrent users without 
performance degradation. 
 
4.4 Security 
• All data transmissions shall be encrypted using HTTPS/TLS. 
• Passwords shall be hashed using a strong one-way hashing algorithm (e.g., bcrypt). 
• Role-based access control shall be enforced server-side. 
• Session tokens shall expire after a configurable inactivity period. 
 
4.5 Maintainability and Scalability 
• The application shall be designed to support multiple institution clients (multi-tenancy 
architecture). 
• The codebase shall follow modular design principles to facilitate future feature 
additions. 
• The system shall support the addition of new campuses and buildings without 
architectural changes. 
 
4.6 Accessibility 
• The interface shall comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards. 
• Text labels and alt-text shall be provided for all map icons and floor plan elements.

5.2 System Architecture Overview 
The system follows a client-server architecture with the following key components: 
• Client Layer: Next.js application rendering UI for web and PWA-enabled mobile 
devices. Service Workers manage offline caching of timetables and map data. 
• API Layer: A Python backend (FastAPI or Django REST Framework) exposes 
RESTful endpoints for authentication, timetable management, hall management, hall 
search, map data, notifications, and user management. Next.js API routes handle 
frontend-proxied calls and server-side rendering concerns. 
• Business Logic Layer: Server-side modules handle timetable constraint solving, 
availability processing, notification dispatching, and access control enforcement. 
• Data Layer: PostgreSQL relational database stores all application data. Prisma ORM 
is used for type-safe database queries. Multi-tenancy is implemented via institution-
scoped data models. 
• External Services: Google Maps Platform for outdoor cartography. Firebase Cloud 
Messaging or Web Push API for push notifications. 
 
5.3 Data Model — Key Entities 
The principal data entities of the system include: 
• Institution: top-level tenant entity containing campuses, departments, and 
configuration. 
• Campus: belongs to an Institution, has a geographic location, and contains Buildings. 
• Building: belongs to a Campus, has a name and address, and contains Floors. Campus Mapping & Timetable Management System  |  CDC v1.0 
Confidential  |  Page 13 of 18 
• Floor: belongs to a Building, has a floor number and an associated floor plan 
image/SVG. 
• Hall: belongs to a Floor, has a name, capacity, type, and availability status. 
• Department: belongs to an Institution, groups Courses and Students by academic 
field. 
• Level: represents an academic year or semester within a Department. 
• Course: belongs to a Department and Level, has a name and weekly hour allocation. 
• User: polymorphic entity covering Student, Staff, Admin, and Registrar actors. Stores 
institutional email, hashed password, role, and associated department/level. 
• Timetable Entry: maps a Course, Hall, Lecturer, time slot, and week to a scheduled 
class. 
• Availability: records a lecturer's declared availability by day and time slot for a given 
week. 
• Notification: stores notification records with target actor type, department, level, and 
message.

7. Constraints and Assumptions 
7.1 Technical Constraints 
• The frontend must be built using React and Next.js as the primary framework. The 
backend must be implemented in Python using FastAPI or Django REST Framework. 
• Outdoor mapping must use Google Maps Platform (API key required). 
• The application must support offline use via PWA service workers. 
• The system must be deployable on standard cloud infrastructure (Vercel, AWS, or 
equivalent). 
 
7.2 Business Constraints 
• The system must support multi-tenancy to serve multiple institutions from a single 
deployment. 
• Student access to timetable features is gated by tuition payment status, which must 
be integrated with the institution's financial records or manually managed by the 
Registrar. 
• Timetable generation must respect all scheduling constraints without manual 
intervention beyond final approval. 
 
7.3 Assumptions 
• Each institution provides accurate and up-to-date course, hall, and staff data upon 
onboarding. 
• Lecturers will consistently declare their availability before the configured weekly 
deadline. 
• The institution has access to Google Maps API credentials. 
• Floor plan drawings will be provided by the institution in a digital format (SVG or high-
resolution image) and uploaded by the Admin during setup. 
• Tuition payment status is maintained by the Registrar or integrated with an external 
finance system.

9. Acceptance Criteria 
The following criteria must be satisfied for the project to be considered complete and accepted: 
 
• All four actor roles (Student, Staff, Admin, Registrar) can log in with auto-generated 
credentials. 
• Students with unpaid tuition see only the map module; students with confirmed fees 
see their full timetable. 
• Campus maps correctly display all campuses of the institution, including those in 
different cities. 
• Floor plans for all buildings are accessible and navigable floor by floor. 
• Clicking on a timetable hall entry navigates the user to the correct floor plan location. 
• The timetable generation engine produces conflict-free schedules respecting all 
defined constraints. 
• Lecturers can declare and update availability; mid-week updates notify the Admin. 
• Admin can manually modify a timetable entry and affected students receive a push 
notification. 
• The application functions correctly in offline mode for maps and timetable viewing. 
• The application is fully functional on Chrome, Firefox, Safari (web) and installable on 
Android and iOS as a PWA. 
• Performance benchmarks are met: map load under 3 seconds, timetable generation 
under 30 seconds.
