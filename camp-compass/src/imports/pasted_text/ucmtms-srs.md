. Introduction 
1.1 Project Overview 
This document constitutes the formal Cahier des Charges (Software Requirements 
Specification) for the University Campus Mapping and Timetable Management System 
(UCMTMS). It describes the functional and non-functional requirements, system architecture, 
actors, constraints, and technical stack for the development of the application. 
The Application name is Camp-Compass.  
1.2 Project Objectives 
The UCMTMS aims to provide an integrated digital platform that fulfills two primary functions: 
• Interactive Campus Mapping: Provide accurate, interactive maps of university 
campuses including outdoor navigation via Google Maps integration and indoor 
navigation via interactive floor plan drawings for each building. 
• Automated Timetable Generation and Viewing: Automate the creation of weekly 
academic timetables, taking into account courses, lecturer availability, hall capacities, 
and scheduling constraints, while providing role-specific views for students and staff. 
1.3 Scope 
The application is designed for deployment in: 
• Universities and higher education institutions 
• Professional training centers 
• Large secondary schools 
The system shall support multi-campus institutions, including campuses located in different 
cities and regions within the same country. The application shall be accessible via web 
browsers and mobile devices (iOS and Android), and shall function in both online and offline 
modes.

3. Functional Requirements 
3.1 Authentication and User Management 
3.1.1 Login 
• All users (Students, Staff, Admin, Registrar) shall authenticate using an institutional 
email address and an auto-generated password. 
• Credentials are generated by the Registrar upon entry of a new member into the 
institution's system. 
• Users shall be able to change their auto-generated password on first login. 
3.1.2 Access Control 
• The system shall enforce role-based access control (RBAC) for all features. 
• Students with an unpaid first tuition instalment shall only have access to the campus 
mapping module. All other features (timetable, notifications) shall be locked until 
payment is confirmed. 
• Staff members shall access their personal schedule and the mapping module. 
• Admin and Registrar actors shall have access to their respective management 
dashboards. 
3.2 Campus Mapping Module 
3.2.1 Outdoor Campus Maps 
• The system shall display interactive maps of all campuses of the institution using the 
Google Maps API. 
• Each campus shall be identifiable and selectable, even if located in different cities or 
regions. 
• The map shall display buildings, entrances, parking areas, and key points of interest 
with labelled markers. 
• Users shall be able to search for specific buildings or locations within a campus. 
3.2.2 Indoor Floor Plans 
• Each building shall have one or more interactive floor plan drawings, one per floor. 
• Floor plans shall be displayed as interactive image-based drawings (SVG or 
annotated raster images). 
• Users shall be able to navigate between floors within a building. 
• Rooms and halls shall be labelled and clickable, showing details such as room name, 
capacity, and type. 
• The system shall support linking a timetable entry directly to its hall's location on the 
floor plan (see section 3.3.6). 
3.2.3 Multi-Campus Support 
• The system shall support institutions with multiple campuses across different cities 
and regions. 
• A campus selector shall allow users to switch between campuses. 
• All mapping data shall be stored and retrieved on a per-campus basis.

3.3 Timetable Management Module 
3.3.1 Timetable Generation 
• The Admin shall be able to trigger weekly timetable generation. 
• The system shall automatically generate timetables taking into account the following 
constraints: 
○ Courses assigned to each department and academic level. 
○ Lecturer availability for the week (as declared by lecturers). 
○ Hall capacity relative to the number of students in the class. 
○ Hall type (e.g., laboratory, lecture theatre, seminar room) matching the course 
requirements. 
○ Absence of scheduling conflicts: a hall cannot be assigned to two courses at the same 
time slot. 
○ Absence of lecturer conflicts: a lecturer cannot teach two courses simultaneously. 
3.3.2 Timetable Viewing — Students 
• Students shall view timetables filtered exclusively to their enrolled department and 
academic level. 
• No cross-departmental or cross-level timetable data shall be visible to students. 
• The timetable shall display: day, time slot, course name, lecturer name, and hall 
designation. 
3.3.3 Timetable Viewing — Staff 
• Staff members who are lecturers shall view their personal weekly teaching schedule. 
• Non-teaching staff shall have access to general campus timetable information 
relevant to their role. 
3.3.4 Lecturer Availability Declaration 
• Lecturers shall declare their availability for the following week before a configurable 
weekly deadline. 
• Lecturers shall also be permitted to edit their availability mid-week (e.g., due to 
unforeseen circumstances). 
• Any mid-week availability change shall trigger an automatic notification to the Admin. 
• The Admin shall manually update the affected timetable entries accordingly. 
• Students of the impacted department and level shall be notified of the timetable 
change. 
3.3.5 Hall Management 
• The Admin shall manage the list of halls, including their name, campus, building, 
floor, capacity, and type. 
• Halls shall be marked as available or unavailable for scheduling. 
• The system shall prevent double-booking of halls.

update in real time as criteria are entered.

accepting query parameters for all supported search criteria. The Python backend 
layer shall implement the filtering logic using parameterised database queries to 
ensure security and performance. 
3.3.7 Map Integration from Timetable 
• From any timetable entry, a student or staff member shall be able to click on the hall 
name to view its location on the campus map. 
• The system shall navigate to the correct campus, building, and floor plan, highlighting 
the relevant hall. 
3.4 Notification Module 
• The system shall support in-app and push notifications. 
• Notification triggers include: 
○ Timetable changes affecting a student's department and level. 
○ Lecturer availability updates received by the Admin. 
○ System announcements from the Admin. 
• Students shall only receive notifications relevant to their department and level. 
3.5 Registration and Onboarding Module 
• The Registrar shall register new students and staff into the system. 
• Upon registration, the system shall auto-generate an institutional email address and 
a temporary password. 
• Registration details shall include: full name, role, department (for students), 
faculty/department (for staff), and academic level (for students). 
• A student's access tier (restricted vs. standard) shall be linked to their tuition fee 
payment status.

4. Non-Functional Requirements 
4.1 Availability and Offline Mode 
• The application shall function in both online and offline modes using Progressive 
Web Application (PWA) techniques and local data caching. 
• Campus maps and the most recently fetched timetable shall be accessible offline. 
• Data synchronisation shall occur automatically when connectivity is restored. 
4.2 Cross-Platform Compatibility 
• The application shall be accessible via all major web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, 
Edge, Safari). 
• The application shall be installable on Android and iOS devices as a PWA. 
• The user interface shall be fully responsive and optimised for mobile screens. 
4.3 Performance 
• Map tiles and floor plan images shall load within 3 seconds on a standard 4G mobile 
connection. 
• Timetable generation shall complete within 30 seconds for a standard institution 
configuration. 
• The application shall support a minimum of 5,000 concurrent users without 
performance degradation. 
4.4 Security 
• All data transmissions shall be encrypted using HTTPS/TLS. 
• Passwords shall be hashed using a strong one-way hashing algorithm (e.g., bcrypt). 
• Role-based access control shall be enforced server-side. 
• Session tokens shall expire after a configurable inactivity period. 
4.5 Maintainability and Scalability 
• The application shall be designed to support multiple institution clients (multi-tenancy 
architecture). 
• The codebase shall follow modular design principles to facilitate future feature 
additions. 
• The system shall support the addition of new campuses and buildings without 
architectural changes. 
4.6 Accessibility 
• The interface shall comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards. 
• Text labels and alt-text shall be provided for all map icons and floor plan elements.
